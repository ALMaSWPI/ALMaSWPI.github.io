About

Mohammad Mahdi Agheli Hajiabadi is an Associate Teaching Professor in the Robotics Engineering Department at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), with a joint appointment in Mechanical Engineering. He leads the Autonomous Loco-Manipulation Systems (ALMaS) research group.

His teaching and research span robotics, dynamics, controls, and intelligent robotic systems, with a strong emphasis on hands-on, project-based learning and close integration between theory and practice.

Office: Unity Hall 285
Email: mmaghelih@wpi.edu

Teaching

Dr. Agheli teaches undergraduate and graduate courses in robotics, kinematics, dynamics, and control systems. His teaching philosophy emphasizes conceptual understanding, critical thinking, and the ability to apply fundamental principles to real-world robotic systems.

Research

His research interests include legged robots, wheeled and mobile robots, soft and hybrid robotic systems, and robotic platforms for construction and manufacturing applications.

Current work within the ALMaS group focuses on mobile manipulation, autonomous construction robots, robotic 3D printing, and the interaction between mechanical design, materials, and autonomy.
