About

I am an Associate Teaching Professor in the Robotics Engineering Department at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), with a joint appointment in Mechanical Engineering. I lead the Autonomous Loco-Manipulation Systems (ALMaS) research group.

My teaching and research focus on robotics, dynamics, and control, with a strong emphasis on project-based learning and the tight integration of theory, experimentation, and real robotic systems.

Office: Unity Hall 285
Email: mmaghelih@wpi.edu

Teaching

Research

My research interests include legged robots, wheeled and mobile robots, soft and hybrid robotic systems, and autonomous robotic platforms for construction and manufacturing applications.

Current work within the ALMaS group focuses on mobile manipulation, robotic 3D printing, autonomous construction systems, and the interaction between mechanical design, materials, and autonomy.
